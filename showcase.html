Showcase

Ink, held to the light

Calligraphy is where Persian art learned to make sound visible — a line drawn at the speed of breath. The works gathered here grew out of the cross-disciplinary projects Bruce Nemeth conceived, directed, and curated, with the brush in the hand of his partner Behnam, artist and calligrapher. Walk slowly; everything here was made slowly.

Room I

Canvases

The classical letterforms leave the page and take on the scale of the body — gestures in red, white, and black, each canvas holding a single breath of movement.

sound before language —
the archive of what stays unsaid

Room II

In the open

The canvases carried out to where the city is oldest — set against brick, dust, and doorways, and photographed in black and white as the street moves past.

Room III

Works on paper

Closest to the tradition: mashq — the practice sheet, written and rewritten until the words dissolve into rhythm. Torn leaves are stitched back together with staples; fragments are collaged; one sheet is framed the classical way.

Wordless

2025 · Colleague Complex Gallery, Shiraz

The inaugural exhibition of the gallery: action calligraphy, live sound installation, and video projection merged into one immersive environment. Soundscapes ran across fourteen calligraphic works; some four hundred visitors passed through in four nights.

Curator, art director & sound design — Bruce Nemeth

Forgotten

2025 · Dalan Restoration Studio, Shiraz

A site-specific installation inside a 200-year-old Qajar-era house: nine Persian mystical words whose meanings have eroded, returned to the rooms through spatialised sound, action calligraphy, and architectural intervention — two nights, a team of fifteen.

Curator, art director & sound design — Bruce Nemeth
